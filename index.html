About FCC Consulting

Felicia Collins Correia as the Principal of FCC Consultation and Training, has over twenty five years of CEO experience and strong executive competency. Ms. Collins Correia oversaw two well established non-profits organizations in Tulsa (Domestic Violence Intervention Services/Call Rape and YWCA Tulsa) each with budgets over 3.5 million dollars. She has experience in the service delivery, fund development and community relations arenas, as well as in growing programs and evaluating service delivery impact.

During her 18 year tenure as the head of Domestic Violence Intervention Services, Ms. Correia served on the Board of the National Network to End Domestic Violence in Washington, DC for six years and served one year as their Board Chair. Seen as a leader in the field, she initiated the grant process which lead DVIS to receive one of the first federally funded Department of Justice Family Safety Center’s in the country. There were hundreds of applicants and only 15 awards given in that first round of funding in 2004. She also was the principal consultant on an initiative spearheaded by the Reynolds Foundation which was considering making a multimillion dollar commitment to fund domestic violence shelters in Oklahoma, Arkansas and Nevada. Ms. Correia worked alongside Foundation senior staff to conduct needs assessments in the three states; wrote up the findings and presented them to the Reynolds Foundation Board of Directors. She also continued to serve as a consultant to the Foundation after the organizations were funded, providing expertise in workshops settings which the Foundation held to build stronger organizational capacity among the programs funded. Following her July, 2014 retirement, Felicia Collins Correia established FCC Consultation and Training, LLC whose primary purpose is to contribute to the community by helping nonprofits and their leaders maximize their impact, leverage their contacts and board volunteers to most effectively implement their mission.

Experience

Since beginning just a year ago, FCC, LLC has assisted a major foundation (Kaiser Family Foundation) in assessing their funding initiates by taking the pulse of community leaders; completed a needs assessment for NCHS in Indiana; worked with a small non-profit to expand their funding base from only public insurance support to receive private foundation dollars; rounding out her consulting by providing coaching and mentoring services to new Executive Directors as well as seasoned CEO’s.

Worthy Services

FCC Service 1

As the principal consultant, FCC Consultation and Training provides a complete assessment of what an organization needs to move to its next stage of development. Organizations have a life cycle, and need strong board support and executive leadership. FCC is able to define what your organization requires for success. FCC has conducted regional programmatic needs assessments as well as strategic planning sessions for nonprofit boards.

FCC Service 2

FCC, as a result of knowledge gained through 26 years of successful executive director leadership of organizations of at least $4,000,000, has mentored CEO’s through transitions, evaluating both their senior staff structures as well as board leadership needs.

FCC Service 3

A seasoned fundraiser, FCC works with the board, evaluating its readiness to conduct a successful fundraising campaign.
